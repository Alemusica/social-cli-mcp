## Logistics: visas, fees, travel, and equipment

### Performance visas

For **Qatar**, there is no specific artist visa. Performers need a standard work visa sponsored by a Qatari employer (hotel, venue, or agency). Processing takes 2–4 weeks; cost is QAR 300 (~€75), typically covered by the employer. Italian citizens get 30-day visa-on-arrival for tourism, but this does not authorize commercial performance. Business visas (up to 3 months) can cover short-term engagements.

The **UAE** offers the most flexible options. The **Cultural Golden Visa** (10 years) is available to musicians and performers — it requires a recommendation letter from Dubai Culture & Arts Authority, portfolio showing 5+ years of artistic experience, and evidence of achievements and awards. Cost is approximately AED 3,000+ (~€750). A **Freelance Permit** through Dubai Creative Clusters Authority enables self-sponsorship. For hotel residencies, the hotel or agency typically sponsors a standard employment visa.

**Saudi Arabia** has a specific **"Visa for Art Workers"** covering musicians performing at events, fairs, and exhibitions. It requires an invitation from the Saudi Ministry of Foreign Affairs and a copy of the Saudi company registration hosting the event. Italian citizens are among the 66 nationalities eligible for tourist eVisa, but commercial performance requires the art worker visa.

Self-contained performers are particularly attractive to venues because they eliminate sound engineer and backline costs. The novelty value of RAV Vast in the Gulf commands a premium over standard solo instruments.

### Travel and equipment

**Direct flights** from Milan Malpensa to Dubai take 6 hours 10 minutes (Emirates operates 3 daily nonstops; Air Arabia and FlyDubai also fly direct). Rome to Dubai is 5 hours 40 minutes (Emirates, ITA Airways). Qatar Airways flies direct from both Rome and Milan to Doha in approximately 5–6 hours. Venice to Doha also has Qatar Airways direct service. Round-trip fares range from **€300–850** depending on season and airline, with budget options available from €125.

FLUTUR's equipment setup is fully transportable as airline luggage. The RAV Vast weighs approximately **5.9 kg** at ~50 cm diameter and fits in a padded case as checked baggage. The Ableton Push 3 (~3 kg) fits easily in carry-on. A guitar can be checked or carried in-cabin (Emirates allows instruments up to 80 cm in overhead bins). A compact PA like a Bose S1 Pro (~7 kg) goes as checked luggage. Total equipment weight falls under 25–30 kg — purchase additional checked baggage rather than shipping.

An **ATA Carnet** is essential for temporary duty-free import of professional equipment into all three countries. Apply through the Italian Chamber of Commerce (Camera di Commercio). The carnet permits duty-free temporary import for up to one year and explicitly covers musical instruments and professional equipment. Without one, UAE requires a cash or bank guarantee equivalent to customs duties (generally 5%). The Dubai Chamber processes ATA Carnets in 1–2 working days.
